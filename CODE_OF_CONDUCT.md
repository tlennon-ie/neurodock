# Code of Conduct

NeuroDock adopts the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) as the base of this Code of Conduct. The sections below are the operative summary; where this file and the Contributor Covenant disagree, the Covenant text governs.

## Our standards

We want NeuroDock to be a project people can contribute to without paying a social tax for being neurodivergent, new, tired, or unfamiliar with the conventions. The following standards apply to everyone — contributors and maintainers alike.

### Behaviours we expect

- Treat other contributors as adults with their own context and constraints.
- Give and accept feedback on the work, not the person.
- State your point plainly. If you do not know, say so.
- Assume the other contributor is acting in good faith until evidence says otherwise.
- Credit prior work, including work that lives outside this repository.
- Respect a contributor's stated capacity, AFK status, or response cadence.
- Use the names and pronouns people give you.
- Apologise plainly when you cause harm, and change the behaviour.

### Behaviours we will not accept

- Harassment, intimidation, or sustained personal attacks.
- Sexualised language or imagery, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Public or private targeting based on neurotype, disability, race, gender, sexuality, religion, or any protected characteristic.
- Doxxing or publishing private information without consent.
- Pathologising another contributor's communication style or pace.
- Demanding a diagnosis, proof of neurotype, or any private medical detail.
- Sustained derailment of issues, pull requests, or .
- Retaliation against a person who reports a violation in good faith.

## ND-aware additions

NeuroDock is built largely by neurodivergent contributors. Three additions reflect that reality and override any conventional reading that contradicts them.

1. **Direct communication is welcomed and not pathologised.** A short, blunt answer is not rudeness. Asking a contributor to soften plain technical language because it "feels harsh" is not a valid Code of Conduct concern.

2. **Requests for explicit clarification are not aggression.** "What exactly do you mean by X?" and "Can you state the acceptance criteria?" are normal contributor speech. Treating these as confrontational is itself a violation.

3. **Async latency is normalised.** No contributor owes immediate availability. A response in days, or weeks, after an AFK declaration is the contract — not an exception. Pinging the same contributor repeatedly for response is not acceptable.

## Scope

This Code applies on every project surface: the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, community chat channels, project events, and any public space where someone represents NeuroDock.

## Enforcement

The project maintainer handles reports. Send reports to `conduct@neurodock.org` (mailbox configured once DNS is live; until then, open a private security advisory on GitHub).

First acknowledgement of a report arrives within five working days. After acknowledgement, the maintainer investigates, hears from the involved parties, and decides on a response within thirty calendar days of the initial report. Responses range from a private clarification to a temporary suspension to permanent removal from project spaces.

The reporter is told the outcome. The subject of the report is told the outcome and the reasoning.

## Appeals

A contributor subject to an enforcement action can appeal once, in writing, within fourteen days of the action. The maintainer reviews the appeal in good faith. The original decision stands unless the maintainer changes it.
