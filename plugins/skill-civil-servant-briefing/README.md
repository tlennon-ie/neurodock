# skill-civil-servant-briefing

A profession-shaped NeuroDock skill for the three things civil servants, policy advisers, and NGO programme staff actually do in their week: draft a structured briefing note, synthesise consultation responses without editorialising, and protect a sitting-day with six or more meetings from premature break nudges. Three trigger phrases, three flows, no overlap with the general-purpose planning skills.

## Who this is for

- **Civil servants** drafting briefing notes for a Minister, Secretary General, or senior official — and tired of templating the five sections from scratch every time.
- **Policy advisers** (Departmental, parliamentary, or thinktank) who synthesise consultation responses for a living and need a structured map that respects the principled disagreements without flattening them.
- **NGO programme staff** writing briefs for a board, a funder, or a programme committee — same five-section shape, different audience.
- **Special advisers and staff in Ministers' offices** who run meeting-marathon days and need the substrate's break-nudges to respect the reality of a sitting-day.

It is **not** for:

- Policy advocacy. The skill produces structurally-neutral artefacts. It does not argue for or against a position, recommend a Departmental line, or generate press lines.
- Ministerial correspondence. Letters from or to a Minister are a different artefact with different conventions; this skill does not write them.
- Stakeholder-influence analysis. The synthesis tells you what was said, not who matters. Influence-weighting is the user's craft.
- Therapy or coaching. The skill helps you structure work; it does not coach you on how to do the work.

## What it does

Three activation paths. The trigger phrase determines the flow.

| Trigger                                              | Flow                  | What it produces                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `draft a briefing note on <topic>, <N> pages`        | Briefing note         | A section-by-section plan in the canonical structure (Background / Current position / Options / Recommendation / Risks), each section sized to the page budget, with atomic write tasks generated by `neurodock-task-fractionator`.  |
| `synthesise stakeholder responses on <consultation>` | Stakeholder synthesis | A structured map of supports / objects / principled disagreements, drawn verbatim from facts recorded in `neurodock-cognitive-graph`. Zero editorial framing.                                                                        |
| `meeting marathon mode`                              | Meeting marathon      | A temporary threshold change for the substrate's chronometric nudges (gentle 90 / nudge 150 / hard 210 minutes), default-restore-at-midnight. For a sitting-day with 6+ meetings where the default thresholds would fire constantly. |

See `SKILL.md` for the full operating instructions and triggers.

## What it explicitly does NOT do

- It does not draft the note itself in flow 1 — only the section plan. Drafting is the follow-on task.
- It does not paraphrase, summarise, or characterise stakeholder positions in flow 2. Quotes verbatim or does not quote.
- It does not weight stakeholders ("influential", "minority view", "outlier") — that is the user's craft.
- It does not editorialise in section headings. The five canonical headings are fixed and carry weight precisely because they are predictable.
- It does not produce political-press-release vocabulary (no "bold", "courageous", "innovative", "world-class", "stakeholders coming together", "balance must be struck").
- It does not extend marathon mode silently past midnight. The default-restore-at-EOD is a hard contract.
- It does not call `record_fact`. The cognitive graph is the user's record, not the skill's logbook.

## Why these three flows together

These are the recurring artefacts a public-service professional produces that benefit from structure but cannot be templated end-to-end. They share a common shape: take messy input (a topic on a Minister's mind, a pile of consultation responses, a calendar full of stacked meetings), produce a structured artefact that the user can act on, then stop. The skill is profession-shaped — it would not make sense to factor each flow into its own plugin, because the user who wants one almost always wants the others in the same working week.

The three flows also share a common ethics commitment: structural neutrality. A briefing note's Background section is what is, not what should be. A stakeholder synthesis quotes positions, it does not adjudicate between them. A marathon-day threshold change is a calibration to reality, not a judgement that the day's meetings are well-organised. The skill is institutional but not bureaucratic — it respects the craft.

## Dependencies

| MCP server                    | Required? | Used for                                                                                                                                                       |
| ----------------------------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `neurodock-task-fractionator` | **Hard**  | The `decompose` tool in flow 1. Without it, the briefing-note plan cannot be section-sized.                                                                    |
| `neurodock-cognitive-graph`   | **Hard**  | `recall_entity` for the consultation and each stakeholder in flow 2. Without it, the synthesis has nothing verbatim to draw from and the skill refuses to run. |
| `neurodock-chronometric`      | Optional  | `mark_session_start` in flow 3 so the substrate's break-suggestion logic has the marathon intent on file. The skill emits the threshold block either way.      |

The two hard requirements are declared in `plugin.yaml` under `requires.mcp_servers`. The chronometric integration is declared in `SKILL.md` frontmatter with `optional: true` and is gated behind an availability check; if missing, the skill still emits the threshold block but the closing line tells the user the substrate's nudges are not currently wired.

## How to install

NeuroDock discovers plugins from two filesystem roots. To install for local testing:

**Per-user (recommended for trying it out):**

Linux:

```bash
mkdir -p ~/.local/share/neurodock/plugins/
cp -r plugins/skill-civil-servant-briefing ~/.local/share/neurodock/plugins/
```

macOS:

```bash
mkdir -p "$HOME/Library/Application Support/neurodock/plugins/"
cp -r plugins/skill-civil-servant-briefing "$HOME/Library/Application Support/neurodock/plugins/"
```

Windows PowerShell:

```powershell
$dest = "$env:APPDATA\neurodock\plugins\skill-civil-servant-briefing"
New-Item -ItemType Directory -Force -Path (Split-Path $dest) | Out-Null
Copy-Item -Recurse plugins\skill-civil-servant-briefing $dest
```

**In-repo (already done if you're reading this from the cloned repo):**

The substrate also scans `<repo>/plugins/*/plugin.yaml`, so cloning this repo is enough — no copy needed.

Restart your LLM client (Claude Desktop, Claude Code). The substrate scans both roots at init.

To verify discovery:

```bash
neurodock plugin list
```

The plugin should appear with `trust: community` and the three triggers listed above.

## How to use

Once installed, just say one of the trigger phrases in your normal LLM client. The skill is profile-agnostic (`neurotypes: []`) so it auto-activates for any user whose profile allows community plugins.

Examples:

> Draft a briefing note on housing supply, 4 pages.

> Synthesise stakeholder responses on the Planning Act consultation.

> Meeting marathon mode.

The skill replies with a section plan, a stakeholder map, or a threshold confirmation (respectively). It does not propose follow-up questions, ask "anything else?", or otherwise extend the conversation. When the artefact is ready, the skill stops.

## When NOT to use it

- **You're drafting Ministerial correspondence.** Letters are a different artefact. This skill does not write them.
- **You want the skill to recommend a Departmental position.** It will not. Either the recommendation is the user's craft, or it goes through a separate decision-making process.
- **You want to characterise which stakeholders are influential.** The synthesis flow refuses to. That is the user's analytical work.
- **You're producing press lines or Ministerial soundbites.** Different tool, different ethics. This skill produces structural artefacts, not political ones.

## Pairing with other skills

- Pair with `adhd-daily-planner` (in-tree) if you want a morning brief that surfaces "you have a briefing-note deadline at 16:00" so you can chain into `draft a briefing note on <topic>, <N> pages`.
- Pair with `audhd-context-recovery` (in-tree) when returning to a consultation you have not looked at in weeks — `where was I on the Planning Act` first, then `synthesise stakeholder responses on the Planning Act consultation`.
- Pair with `skill-eng-manager-1on1`'s blameless-postmortem flow if a programme delivery has gone wrong and the resulting briefing note's Risks section needs to draw on the postmortem skeleton without naming a person.

## Tests

Three replayable conversation tests in `tests/` cover the three flows end-to-end. Same format as `plugins/skill-eng-manager-1on1/tests/`. Run them with:

```bash
neurodock skill test plugins/skill-civil-servant-briefing
```
