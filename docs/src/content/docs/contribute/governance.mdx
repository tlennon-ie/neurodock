NeuroDock is governed by a small maintainer council with rotating membership and a separate clinical advisory board. Lived experience leads. Diagnosis is never a gate.

## Maintainer council

- Five seats.
- Two-year staggered terms.
- At least three seats reserved for contributors with relevant lived experience (self-identified).
- Decisions by simple majority on routine matters; consensus required on changes affecting manifesto, ethics, or governance itself.
- Documented escalation path when consensus fails.

## Clinical advisory board

- Five members, two-year staggered terms.
- Composition target: at least one psychologist with ND specialism, one CBT/ERP practitioner, one occupational therapist, plus two practitioners with relevant lived experience.
- Quarterly review of detector heuristics, thresholds, and the published position paper.
- **Advisory — not veto.** The council decides. Advisors advise.

## Lived-experience-first review

`CODEOWNERS` ensures skills targeted at a specific neurotype are reviewed by at least one contributor with that neurotype. Self-identification is sufficient. Diagnosis is never required and never asked for.

If you self-identify with a neurotype and would like to join the lived-experience reviewer pool, open an issue tagged `lived-experience-reviewer`. The community-triage agent picks it up.

## Burnout protocol

- Maintainers can declare AFK with no questions asked.
- Reviews automatically reassign when a maintainer is AFK.
- Every package has at least two CODEOWNERS. No solo bus factors.
- Quarterly retros include an explicit "how is everyone holding up" agenda item.

## ND-aware code of conduct

Beyond CC4 base:

1. Direct communication is welcomed and not pathologised.
2. Requests for explicit clarification are not interpreted as aggression.
3. Latency in async response is normalised; no contributor owes immediate availability.

## Funding

GitHub Sponsors and Open Collective from day one. No commercial paid tier from the project itself. Long-term sustainability through supporter-company partnerships (paying for advisory hours, not features) and the Anthropic Builders grant programme once Phase 1 is shipping.
