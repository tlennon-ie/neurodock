# Manifesto

NeuroDock is an open-source, local-first cognitive substrate for neurodivergent professionals, built with — not for — the people who need it.

## Five principles

These five principles govern every architectural, design, governance, and product decision we make. They are non-negotiable. If a change conflicts with one of them, the change loses.

### 1. Lower friction for users, and for contributors

Every install, every pull request, every new skill is addable in under fifteen minutes. The cost of participation is the cost of contribution, and we keep both low. When a contributor cannot find the on-ramp, that is our bug, not theirs.

### 2. Local-first by default; cloud is opt-in

Neurodivergent telemetry never leaves the user's machine without an explicit consent action per scope. The default install runs without any network calls beyond the user's chosen LLM client. Cloud features exist, but the user turns them on deliberately and sees a visible indicator while they are active.

### 3. Lived experience leads

Reviewers with the relevant neurotype have final say on artefacts targeted at that neurotype. Self-identification is sufficient — diagnosis is never a gate, and we never ask for one. Three of five council seats are reserved for contributors with lived experience, and `CODEOWNERS` routes neurotype-specific work to neurotype-specific reviewers.

### 4. Composable over monolithic

Profiles, skills, MCP servers, and plugins are swappable units. No god-modules, no hidden coupling, no forced bundles. A contributor adds value by writing one small thing that works, not by understanding the whole.

### 5. Refuse where appropriate

We build clinical guardrails into the substrate. AI that fuels rumination, hyperfocus, or anxiety is a regression, not a feature. Every guardrail is transparent, user-configured, and overridable — but it exists, and it fires.

## What we are not

NeuroDock is not a treatment, a diagnosis tool, or a substitute for clinical care. It is not a substitute for workplace accommodations, and it does not measure productivity in hours, lines of code, or throughput. It is a tool. The user decides what they want from it, and the user keeps that decision.

## An invitation

This project is built by neurodivergent contributors using the very system being built. If that describes you — ADHD, autistic, OCD, AuDHD, dyslexic, or any combination — your lived experience is the work, not a credential to prove. Read `CONTRIBUTING.md`, pick an on-ramp, and pace yourself. We are here for the long version.
